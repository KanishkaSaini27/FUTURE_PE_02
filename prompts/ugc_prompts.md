# Prompts — UGC Ad Content for ClearSkin Face Wash
**Task 2 | Future Interns Prompt Engineering Track**

---

## Prompt 1 — Hook Generation

```
Act as a UGC ad scriptwriter who specializes in skincare products.

Product: ClearSkin Acne & Pimple Face Wash
Target audience: People aged 16-28 dealing with acne/breakouts who have
tried multiple products without success

Generate 5 scroll-stopping opening hooks (first 1-2 lines of a UGC video)
for this product. Each hook should:
- Sound like a real person talking, not an ad
- Create curiosity or relate to a frustration
- Avoid generic marketing words like "transform", "revolutionary", "amazing"
- Be short enough to say in 3-4 seconds on camera

Format: numbered list, one line each.
```

### Why I wrote this prompt this way:
My first version didn't say "avoid generic marketing words" and the hooks came back sounding like ad copy — phrases like "transform your skin in days." Adding that constraint fixed it completely; the second batch sounded much more like something a real person would actually say on camera.

## Prompt 2 — Full UGC Script (Problem → Solution → CTA)

```
Act as a UGC ad scriptwriter for skincare products.

Product: ClearSkin Acne & Pimple Face Wash
Format: 30-45 second UGC video script (as if talking directly to camera)

Write 3 different full scripts following this structure:
1. Problem (relatable acne frustration — be specific, not generic)
2. Discovery (how they found the product, casually, not salesy)
3. Solution/Result (what changed, with a believable timeframe)
4. CTA (soft, not pushy — like a friend recommending something)

Tone: Authentic, slightly imperfect, like a real customer — not a brand voice.
Avoid sounding scripted. Include natural pauses or filler phrasing where it
makes sense.
```

### Why I wrote this prompt this way:
I asked for "believable timeframe" specifically because my first draft had someone seeing clear skin in 2 days, which felt fake and would actually hurt trust. I also added "include natural pauses" because the very first script sounded too clean and corporate — real UGC has small imperfections.

## Prompt 3 — Platform Adaptation

```
Take the 3 UGC scripts written for ClearSkin Acne & Pimple Face Wash and
adapt them for 3 different platforms:

1. Instagram Reels — slightly longer, can include a more personal story arc
2. YouTube Shorts — punchier, faster pacing, hook must land in first 2 seconds
3. Facebook Ads — slightly more direct, can be a touch more sales-aware since
   Facebook audiences expect more obvious ads than Instagram/TikTok-style feeds

Keep the core message the same, but adjust pacing, length, and directness
for each platform.
```

### Why I wrote this prompt this way:
I didn't realize how different platform expectations actually are until I ran this — Facebook audiences are more tolerant of "ad-like" content, while Reels/Shorts need to feel native to the feed or people scroll past immediately. Asking the AI to explain platform differences (not just rewrite) helped me understand the reasoning, not just get the output.

## Prompt 4 — Captions & CTA Variations

```
Write short Instagram/Facebook captions (2-3 lines each) to pair with the
3 UGC scripts for ClearSkin Acne & Pimple Face Wash.

Also generate 5 CTA variations ranging from soft ("worth checking out tbh")
to direct ("link in bio, 20% off today only") so I can choose based on
funnel stage — cold audience vs. warm/retargeting audience.
```

### Why I wrote this prompt this way:
I wanted CTA options at different intensity levels because using a hard-sell CTA on a cold audience (people seeing the brand for the first time) usually performs worse than a softer one — that's something I picked up while researching how UGC ads typically work before writing this prompt.
