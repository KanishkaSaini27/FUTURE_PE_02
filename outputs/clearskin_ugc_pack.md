## 1. Project Overview

This document is the final content deliverable for Task 2 of the Future Interns Prompt Engineering internship. It contains a complete UGC (User Generated Content) style ad pack for a fictional D2C skincare brand, **ClearSkin**, built around their Acne & Pimple Face Wash.

I picked this product because skincare is one of the biggest UGC ad categories right now — almost every D2C skincare brand runs creator-style content instead of polished commercials, because audiences trust "real" testimonials far more than obvious advertising. Acne also has a strong emotional angle (frustration, self-consciousness, relief), which made it a good test case for writing hooks and scripts that feel authentic rather than salesy.

## 5. Platform-Adapted Versions {#5-platform-adapted-versions}

*Generated using Prompt 3 — see `prompts/ugc_prompts.md`*

### Instagram Reels (Script 1, adapted)
Slightly longer, personal story arc retained. Voiceover paired with before/after style clips, talking head intro, product close-up mid-video. Full version of Script 1 works as-is for Reels — Instagram audiences tolerate a slower build-up if the story feels genuine.

### YouTube Shorts (Script 1, adapted — punchier)
"I never talk about my skin but I have to. (pause) I've tried everything for acne — nothing worked until ClearSkin. Three weeks in, my breakouts went from constant to barely-there. Genuinely worth trying. Link below."
*(Hook must land in first 2 seconds — front-loaded the strongest line, cut the slower build-up from the original script.)*

### Facebook Ads (Script 2, adapted — more direct)
"I used to think skincare ads were all fake — until my own skin proved me wrong. After 2-3 weeks of using ClearSkin's Acne & Pimple Face Wash, my breakouts noticeably reduced and my skin texture improved. If you've struggled with acne for years like I did, this is genuinely worth trying. Shop now — link below."
*(Facebook audiences expect more obvious "ad" framing, so the CTA is more direct and the brand name is mentioned earlier.)*

## 7. CTA Variations by Funnel Stage {#7-cta-variations-by-funnel-stage}

| CTA | Intensity | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| "Worth checking out tbh." | Very Soft | Cold audience — first-time viewers, no prior brand awareness |
| "Genuinely changed my skin, link in bio if you want to try it." | Soft | Cold/warm audience — slight personal recommendation |
| "If you're tired of trying everything, just give this one a shot." | Medium | Warm audience — people already showing interest |
| "Shop ClearSkin now — link below." | Direct | Warm/retargeting — people who've engaged with the brand before |
| "Link in bio, 20% off today only." | Very Direct | Retargeting/urgency — warm audience close to converting |

## 8. Conclusion

This project demonstrates how a structured prompt engineering workflow — hook generation, full script writing with an emotional arc, platform-specific adaptation, and funnel-aware CTA variation — can produce a complete, ready-to-use UGC ad content pack rather than a single generic ad script.

The biggest lesson from this task was that authenticity in UGC content comes from explicit constraints, not just asking for "casual" tone. Telling the AI to avoid marketing buzzwords, include believable timeframes, and add small imperfections made a noticeable difference between content that read as an obvious ad versus content that actually sounded like a real customer talking.

**Note:** This is fictional content created for a fictional brand (ClearSkin) for internship purposes. Any real skincare advertising claims would need legal and compliance review before being used in actual paid ads, since skincare/health claims are regulated in most countries.
